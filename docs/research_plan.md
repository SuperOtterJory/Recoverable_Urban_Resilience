# Research Plan

## Paper Claim

The paper introduces recoverable urban resilience as a decision-sensitive property of urban recovery: not merely whether a city rebounds, but how much observed functional loss can be reduced by constrained, timely, and equitable intervention.

## Data-Mining Questions

1. Is there observable functional loss in the data?
2. Are rainfall or other external shocks temporally aligned with mobility-speed degradation?
3. Do cities differ in severity, recovery speed, and exposure concentration?
4. Can demand and network data define functional dependence `Q_t` or at least a baseline dependence proxy?
5. Can observed recovery dynamics calibrate endogenous recovery `A_t`?
6. Which claims require counterfactual optimization rather than descriptive mining?

## Evidence Needed Before Modeling

- Cross-city coverage table.
- Rainfall shock characterization.
- Speed-deficit time-series metrics.
- Recovery-rate or recovery-time proxies.
- Demand/network structure metrics.
- Mapping from observed data columns to model parameters.
- A scored assessment of whether the data can support the conceptual contribution.
