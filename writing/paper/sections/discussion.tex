The analysis distinguishes what the data can identify directly from what requires counterfactual modeling. Descriptive data mining reveals rainfall timing, speed-deficit abnormality, endogenous recovery, spatial concentration, and OD functional dependence. It cannot by itself identify the additional loss that would have been reduced by unobserved interventions. The optimization model supplies this counterfactual probe, and the learning analysis then compresses the optimizer-derived value field into interpretable laws.

The resulting laws are conditional rather than universal constants. They are learned under a standardized recovery regime: fixed intervention primitives, effectiveness assumptions, costs, deployment caps, response delays, budget intensity, and piecewise-linear diminishing returns. Within that regime, the findings are stable: first-order marginal value follows an activated exposure--horizon--efficiency form, while complete finite-budget allocation requires residual re-scoring to avoid saturation and overlapping effects. The representative scenario-optimum validation further shows that this residual law remains close to non-base LP optima when budget and delay change.

The main empirical message is therefore not that the largest disruptions are always the most recoverable. In the current sample, event-level decision-criticality is more closely tied to marginal-value top-tail concentration than to no-intervention loss magnitude. This supports a decision-centered definition of recoverable resilience: events are most valuable for management when addressable loss is concentrated on a small set of exposed, intervention-sensitive locations.

Several boundaries remain. The current sample contains seven full-zone cities with usable speed-event overlap. Scenario-specific LP closure is representative rather than exhaustive because several hard non-base cases reached the solver time limit. Intervention effectiveness and cost parameters are not causally identified from observed intervention records. Finally, the present surrogate is intentionally interpretable and lightweight; a graph neural structure extractor and perturbed-optimum stability analysis would strengthen the AI law-discovery component in a later version.
